Reality Check: $100 Across 10 Years

Consumer Price Index (CPI)
This visualisation shows how Australia's Consumer Price Index (CPI) has changed over the past decade, reflecting movements in the overall cost of living. The CPI is calculated by the Australian Bureau of Statistics using a fixed "basket" of goods and services that households commonly purchase, including essentials like food, housing, transport, healthcare, and recreation. A higher CPI means prices have risen compared to the base period, reducing purchasing power each dollar buys less than before. Over time, changes in CPI are used as a key measure of inflation. In Australia, inflation remained relatively stable for much of the past decade before rising sharply due to disruptions such as the COVID-19 pandemic, supply chain pressures, and higher energy and housing costs. This chart helps illustrate how those economic shifts translate into real changes in the cost of everyday life.
Wage Price Index (WPI)
This visualisation compares wage growth trends across the same period. The WPI tracks changes in the price of labour in Australia, helping to reveal whether wages have kept pace with the rising cost of living.
State CPI Comparison
This choropleth map compares CPI values across Australian states and territories. Darker shades indicate higher inflation levels, highlighting regional disparities in the cost of living across the country.
